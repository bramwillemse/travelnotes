Colombia

Cartagena

Diving Planet

Do a diving mini course if you have never done it before. If so: diving with them is great too. Awesome coral reefs, fishes etc. The owner is cool, great example of a smart, internationally oriented Columbian entrepreneur.

Hostel “Mama Waldy”

is located in historic Cartagena. The street has awesome street art and the hostel itself has some airco-rooms. Great atmosphere!

Restaurante Coroncoro

If you like chicken, try their Arroz con Pollo (with patacones instead of potatoes!) and a lovely refreshing agua de panella (lemonade)

Minca (near Santa Martha)

Take a collectivo from Santa Marta to Minca (ask a cab driver where you can find them, they’ll lead you there)

Casa Loma

might be the most beautiful hostel I’ve ever stayed at. The climb (with all your luggage) up to the hill is totally worth it, because when you reach the top you’re welcomed with a fresh glass of water and a hammock or chair to catch your breath. At that moment we were immediately presented with one of the best sunsets of our trip!

Take a hike to Los Pinos, with a great view towards the back country (mountains) on one side, Santa Marta and the Carribean sea on the other.

Check out the ‘tour’ at the small cacao/coffee finca at the entrance of the village (as seen from Santa Marta). It’s small and simple, but the owner’s a great guy and will tell you a lot about how they grow and produce coffee & cacao. Be sure to bring some of their honey :)

There’s a big cacao finca too where you can get a tour. Should be interesting too, but we didn’t go there.

Watch out when you ride on a mototaxi. Their exhausts will burn your legs when you get on or off! (Jenny got a nasty burn wound..).
